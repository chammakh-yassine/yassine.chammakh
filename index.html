Big picture of what i learned
Course: VHB (Virtual Hochschule Bayern) - German IT Preparation Course

I recently completed a specialized course offered by the Virtual Hochschule Bayern (VHB), conducted entirely in German to enhance my language skills. This preparatory IT course was designed for academic studies in Germany and covered a comprehensive range of topics, including:
- Mathematics: Fundamental concepts essential for IT.
- Automata Theory: Understanding abstract machines and computational problems.
- Natural Language Processing (NLP): Basics of processing and analyzing natural language data.
- Probability Theory: Principles of probability and their applications in computing

The programming component of the course focused exclusively on Java, providing an in-depth understanding of:

- Java Basics: Core syntax and programming constructs.
- Object-Oriented Programming (OOP): Concepts such as classes, objects, inheritance, polymorphism, and encapsulation.
- Class Diagrams: Visualizing the structure of object-oriented systems.

This course has significantly enhanced my proficiency in Java and prepared me for further academic pursuits in IT.

Course: Foundations: Data, Data, Everywhere (Google/Coursera)

I completed the "Foundations: Data, Data, Everywhere" course by Google, hosted on Coursera. This course provided a comprehensive introduction to the world of data and data analysis. Key learnings included:
- SMART Questions: Formulating questions that are Specific, Measurable, Action-oriented, Relevant, and Time-bound.
- Gap Analysis: Identifying and addressing gaps in processes or knowledge.
- Analytical Thinking: Developing skills in visualization, strategic thinking, problem orientation, correlation analysis, and understanding the big picture.

significant part of the course focused on the data life cycle and the phases of data analysis:

- Ask: Defining the problem and identifying data requirements.
- Prepare: Collecting and organizing data.
- Process: Cleaning and transforming data for analysis.
- Analyze: Interpreting data to draw meaningful insights.
- Share: Presenting findings to stakeholders.
- Act: Using insights to inform decision-making and take action.
